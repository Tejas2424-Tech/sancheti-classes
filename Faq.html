Sancheti Sir on Child Development & Psychology

Drawing on years of classroom experience, Sancheti sir addresses questions parents often ask about raising and guiding their children through school life.

Home is a child's first classroom, and the values learned there shape everything that follows. That's why, in Sancheti sir's experience, parents hold the most pivotal role in a child's overall development.

It's an easy criticism to make, but Sancheti sir — a full-time teacher and parent himself — points out that what matters isn't the number of hours, it's consistency. Setting aside even an hour a day to genuinely engage with a child's daily life builds trust and keeps them motivated far more than time alone would.

As children grow physically and mentally, their relationships with each parent naturally shift and evolve. Rather than trying to win them back with stories of sacrifice or hardship, parents get better results by meeting their child at their current stage and simply giving them the comfort and space they need.

  • Prioritise nutrition — sprouts, protein-rich milk, and soaked dry fruits.
  • Encourage outdoor sports for all-round personality development.
  • Foster healthy competition in a stress-free environment.
  • Focus on self-improvement rather than comparison with others.
  • Keep communication open at home, without discussing finances in front of children.
  • Give children their own space.
  • Spend quality time together at dinner, discussing general awareness and current affairs.
  • Involve children in important family decisions.
  • Hold up positive role models for children to look up to.
  • Plan a family outing at least once a fortnight.

This is a common and natural reaction. New environments bring unfamiliar social pressures that take time to settle, much like adults feel when they start a new job. Sancheti sir recommends visiting the new school regularly until the child feels comfortable and settled.

Low energy in the warmer months can sometimes be helped with a vitamin C supplement. However, if a child seems to disregard teachers or takes punishment lightly rather than as a deterrent, Sancheti sir advises that it's worth consulting a child psychologist for a proper assessment.

Sancheti sir is glad and humbled when past students and their families speak well of the classes. That said, every child's journey is unique, so past students' results aren't a fair benchmark for a new one — each student can prove themselves with the right effort. New students are always welcome to join and work toward their own success.
